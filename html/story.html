Prologue

They say memories shape who you are. But what happens when they’re taken away?

A lost boy finds himself in a land unfamiliar to him, with no memories and no past, faced with questions and choices that will shape the end of his story.

But far beyond, in a distant land unknown to everyone, a storm begins to rise.

Chapter One: The Lost Boy

Near a calm river, a boy woke up, wounded, drenched, and trembling. His clothes were soaked, torn in places, and stained with dirt and blood. He tried to remember… anything. Who he was. What had happened. Where he was. But nothing came.

After a long silence, he pulled himself up and began to follow the path by the river. Every step hurt. His wound throbbed, and his body felt weak. He was hungry. He was thirsty. And he was lost.

After walking for some time, he saw two men riding toward him on horses. They wore armor. Soldiers, perhaps. The boy froze.

One of them asked, “Are you from Growlith?”
